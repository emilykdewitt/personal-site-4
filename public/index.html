About Me

When I was a little girl, I dreamed of being a teacher.

At the time, I didn't fully consider the details of the job like planning lessons, managing behavior, and scheduling my own bathroom breaks, but I loved to learn and share what I had learned with others.

Lucky for me, my dream came true! ... kind of. After graduating from Clemson University, I taught Spanish and worked in school administration for four years. When I moved to Nashville in 2016 to work at a computer science-based high school, the incredible work of our student programmers and teachers planted a seed in my mind and heart. That seed slowly sprouted over the following two years as I taught myself basic programming languages. I love learning and practicing Spanish and Portuguese, and found that coding helped me exercise that same part of my brain.

In 2019, I enrolled in Nashville Software School, where I am currently studying to become a full stack web developer. My career dream has evolved over time, but I look forward to using my love of learning and teaching to help create applications that will help others access and use information in interesting and meaningful ways.
